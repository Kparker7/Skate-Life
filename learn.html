Getting Started

Welcome to your skateboarding journey! Whether you're stepping on a board for the first time or picking it up after years, this page will guide you with essential beginner techniques, safety advice, and skill building steps to build confidence.

Beginner Tips

  • Find your stance: Are you regular (left foot forward) or goofy (right foot forward)? Try both and see what feels natural.
  • Learn to balance: Before riding, just practice standing on your board on a flat surface.
  • Master the push: Use your back foot to push off the ground and keep your front foot steady.
  • Practice turning: Lean gently in the direction you want to turn — carving builds control!
  • Start slow: Use smooth, safe pavement or a skatepark's beginner section to practice.

Safety First

Skateboarding is fun, but safety is key. Always wear proper gear to protect yourself while learning:

  • Helmet a must-have for every skater
  • Knee pads, elbow pads, and wrist guards
  • Closed toe shoes with flat, grippy soles
  • Skate in areas free of traffic and hazards
