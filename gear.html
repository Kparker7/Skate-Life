Types of Gear You’ll Need

  • Deck: The wooden board is usually between 7.5” to 8.5” wide, this can provide the platform to ride on.
  • Trucks: Metal parts mounted underneath the deck that hold the wheels and allow turning.
  • Wheels: Typically polymers; size and hardness affect speed and grip.
  • Bearings: Allow your wheels to spin, the quality matters the most.
  • Grip tape: Sandpaper like surface for your feet to stay secured on the board.
  • Protective gear: Helmets, knee/elbow pads, and wrist guards are essential for safety.

Buying Tips

Start with mid range gear that’s reliable and durable. High end boards are great for pros, but beginners should prioritize comfort and control. Local skate shops can provide personal recommendations and help with setup.
